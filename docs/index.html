Introduction

If you are tired of voting for a candidate and then to realize that once elected that he/she does not hold the promises and engagements he/she defended during the campaign, the following political system might interest you.

To avoid such pitfall, we should thus not elect persons, but just the "ideas" the candidates pretend they would defend. The following is a proposal of such system and as a proposal is presented for feedback, comments and adaptations, in order to be improved.

For brevity the political system hereafter described will be referred as Government By Ideas or in short GBI.

About the Author

The author is a simple French citizen, employee in the private sector and not linked with any political party nor part of any lobby.

Concept

GBI aim is to provide an enhancement to democracy that puts the people closer to decisions having them involved in more varied manners and more frequently than one vote every 4 or 5 years. As a side effect GBI should avoiding lobbies deciding in secret in the place of citizens, it should also lead the fight against corruption, placing the general interest of the state at the center of the government actions. Though lobbies have a place in this political system beside policical parties, associations, corporations and any other subset of citizen whatever is its form sharing some common interests.

Scope

GBI is not a substitute for justice, for police, for education, for healthcare, nor any other regalian services.

GBI's target is to have laws modified, created and withdrawn in order to adapt them to new realities a country is facing. It thus takes the role of a legislative authority and also partially of an executive authority. But GBI has not the role to enforce laws, which stays in the hands of police and justice.

As shortly explained previously, in addition to be a legislative authority, GBI is partial an executive authority. Why partial? Because GBI decision process need time and is not adapted to actions that must be taken quickly like defense, war, fight against terrorism and any other new situation that may arise putting the country at risk *and* needing fast decision. But action that engages the country regarding other countries (cooperation treaties, public foreign affairs) must not be excluded from GBI decision system. For that reason this is only a partial executive power where a president function is still needed and will have to be elected as per democracy usage.

Risk: risk is that such president or a group of person behind him take over the GBI by invoking imminent danger (like mass destruction weapons in another country) to take action that else would be under the scope of GBI

Elections Process

Ideas election

GBI means to not elect candidates but to elect ideas. Ideas have first to be defined and presented. This first step is open to any voter without restriction, this is the idea not its author that matters at this step. We will see below the difference between an voter and a citizen.

But to avoid the system to be overloaded under ideas, each submission should cost a symbolic fixed amount of money plus an amount proportional to the revenue of the submitter. Such amount should allow any working citizen even the ones with the smallest legal revenue to pay the submission of one idea per year and still having enough money to satisfy the minimum level of expenses required to live with dignity (the exact amount of this fee is out of the scope of this document). The number of idea a person can present per year should probably not to be limited as we might have very good an fruitful contributors as well also as sober but less poor ones. But we will see that useless or inadapted ideas will just be ignored GBI process and will not cost much resource to anyone except its author.

Once an idea has been submitted to the system it receives an Idea ID that identifies it and will be used to refer to it in particular for voting.An idea will be retained if a majority of voter approves it. Abstention is considered the same as rejecting an idea, but explicitly voting against an idea is possible and has some interest which will be detailed later. In consequence explicitly voting for a given idea shall not be mandatory.

Risk: here the risk is that no idea would get enough vote for the country to pass new laws in order to adapt to new needs. This is however a good mean to lead people to be concerned by the life of their country for their problems to get solved. To be representative and thus valid, an idea cannot be adopted if it has not been approved by at least half of the votersthis clause is fundamental for GBI. Note that we speak about voters not citizens, how a citizen can become an voter is described below.

This way, limiting the number of idea per person and per time is not necessary as they will just be ignored if their submitter has either not put the means to make it known, understood and accepted, or if the submitted idea is just silly, useless, not interesting. No effort will be needed to have refused (or ignored) ideas purged from the GBI system.

The delay between voting and idea submission, is the time slice during which the idea is exposed to debate, we will designate this period by exposure time. This is the duty and responsibility of the ideas's author to define the proper exposure time duration when he will submit his idea to the system. He will have to choose it in a way to maximize the number of votes and still not delay the idea too much in the future for it can be taken by voters as a solution to the current problems.

This is thus the duty of an idea submitter to make it known, understood, voted for and accepted. All mean are possible for that, including advertisement, social networks, emails... Debates could take place anywhere, usenet newsgroups, public meetings, emails, social media, whatever, but always clearly indicating the idea ID's they debate about, compare, valuate, promote or criticize. However unfair communication means that do not allow anyone to present its idea (TV broadcast, newspaper... in particular when privately owned), should not be allowed to mention exposed ideas or votable ideas and their responsible should be fined in case they would refer directly (Idea ID) or indirectly (title, tag, keywords) to an idea. The amount of the fine should be double at each recurrence.

Risk: the reason why to forbid unfair communication means to promote ideas takes it root in the will to reduce the risk of citizen brain washing by owners of such communication means (TV, newspaper...), omitting to mention alternatives to ideas not in phase with their own ideology. This reason behind that is to reduce the hidden scope a lobby can have to influence voter's voting and thus decisions taken for the country, when it is the owner of such communcation mean and defines the editorial line.

Once the exposition delay has been passed, the voting phase starts at the end of which the idea either adopted (if it has a majority of votes) or rejected. There is no need to synchronize the time and date of votes between different ideas, we could speak about continuous voting.

Risk: the strongest risk here is that GBI strongly need computer science based systems. Especially for voting, unlike physical vote where any citizen can check that there is no fraud in each vote center, and seen the publicly available result of each vote center checking that the result of its vote center is correctly reported and that the global vote is true (but how many citizen take the time to do that today?). Using computer science means completely removing the citizen control against fraud. A voting system is proposed in annex that leverage Internet access and tend to minimize if not avoiding fraud.

We could also consider to not distinguish the exposure time from the voting time, but this would imply to allow any voter to change is mind and thus of vote before the end of the common period of exposure and voting, whil still preserving the voting secrecy. (An example of system that allows such freedom is described in annex)

It could also be defined that when the time remaining before end of vote is only a third of the total exposition time (for example), voters could not change their vote anymore, only voter not having yet voted could add their voice to or against the concerned idea.

Having the information of explicites votes for or against each ideas currently available for voting could be a possible option, it will however become necessary for amendments (see below). In addition, providing a list of the most voted idea still in voting phase (nothing says whether votes are favorable or not) could be interesting to compensate the 50 % minimum votes an idea has to obtain to be accepted.

Super-Ideas

An idea can be dependent on another one. Each idea will thus have a dependency list (eventually void), but cyclic dependency (idea A depends on idea B which itself depends on idea A) cannot be possible due to the fact an idea is identified at the time it is registered and then becomes immutable, thus it can only depend on ideas that existed at the time it had been registered. We will designate by super-idea any such idea that relies on at least one other idea. In the same way, we will designate an leaf-idea, an idea that does not depend on any other idea.

A new idea could thus list a set of other existing ideas (by their idea's ID) and voting for it will imply voting for all ideas it depends on. Of course an elector could not vote for an idea if this one has in its dependency list an idea the voter has already explicitly voted against. However the elector can still vote for any other idea presented in the dependency list of this super-idea, but not for the super-idea itself. This way an elector could vote against some ideas (that he find unacceptable) and see what's left to vote for.

A super-idea could also be listed in the dependency list of another idea. This way, a given idea could be referred in the dependency list of many other ideas, directly or indirectly, making a tree of ideas.

In addition to that, an idea could thus be referred by several different super idea trees making common branches between these trees.

We could imagine political party providing their own super-ideas and their set of dependent ideas. We could also imagine some other political party borrowing some sub-ideas from a first party by adding them in the dependency list of their master super-idea (directly or not). Though any citizen could decide to vote for any leaf-idea whatever is the party or citizen it has been submitted by, as well as voting for super-ideas that have this leaf-idea in their dependency list, leading the ideas common to different political party to have more chance to be elected.

Debates and amendments

Debates

Minister

Now that an idea has been retained thanks to the idea election process described above, a person has to be selected (not elected, but randomly chosen - see chapter for that topic) to implement it. Any people that has the right to vote and has not been condemned for corruption in their live can postulate to execute a given idea. The selected candidate will be called the minister for this idea. A person cannot be minister for two different ideas at the same time. But a person can be minister in his life as many times as he has postulated and has been selected.

The role of the minister is be to implement the idea, that's it to define all the necessary for this idea to become applicable in the perimeter described by this idea submitter. He will also have the duty to regularly report about his actions: what has been done so far, what stays to be done, what are the problems met today to go forward, and so on. The frequency of these status report are defined at the submission time of the idea by its author.

If a super-idea has been elected, a minister for this super-idea will have the duty to implement the whole tree of dependent ideas (see Ministerial Cabinet below). There will not be minister selection for any of the child idea of this super-idea.

However if two elected super-ideas have the same idea in their dependency list, or depend on the same branch of ideas (a common branch of ideas between two elected trees), this common sub-idea or common branch of ideas they depend on will have a dedicated minister and will be removed from the responsibility of the each super-idea ministers. If any such super-idea need first such sub-idea or sub-branch to be implemented in order to start, the minister could decide to postpone its execution, the same way as he can decide to temporarily suspend its execution, and decide of of its end (successful or failed). In any case, he will have to justify his decision as defined by the idea submitter at least during the planned action status reports.

Censors

In parallel to minister candidates, censors have to be selected (randomly chosen from the list of censor candidates) for a given idea to control that the actions status provided by the minister are true and sincere in regard to actions effectively performed, encontered constraints, realized objectives,and so on. Censor will be able to trigger Justice if there is significant difference between actions status report done by the minister and reality of facts, or if the action performed would be outside the perimeter of the original idea. A minister could be this way revoked and condemned. He would not be allowed anymore to postulate for a role of minister for the rest of his life. If the judgment does not retain the minister to be guilty, the censor should not be prosecuted, unless he triggered justice without strong enough reasons to do so. In that case he may be condemned for censorship abuse. A censor could be condemned up to three time for abuse in his life (being censor for the same idea or for another one), after that he won't be able to be selected as censor for the rest of his life.

Risk: if censors do not feel free to investigate and if necessary, to trigger the Justice, the risk is that this role will serve no purpose. However a mechanism must exist to prevent abuse. The fact the censor is randomly chosen for a given idea reduces the risk but does not remove it completely that a censor considers its role more in regard to the selected minister person rather than in regard to the action he performs (opposite/different political party or human conflict), or worse in regard to the nature of the idea itself (idea the censor does not like would receive a lot of "investigations" against the minister, or at the opposite, an idea the censor approves would receive little or no control for its execution...).

If a super-idea has been elected, there will not be a super-censors, but as many censor as there is ideas and sub-ideas. This rule will make sense with minister cabinet described below. The censorship resource must not be reduced for the level of control to be independent from the super-idea hierarchy and selected minister(s) that implement them.

Note: An alternative to having a censor per idea is to define professionals/officials independent from the number of ideas. The history showed that such public human resources tend to be reduced over time in the expenses reduction plans. The consequences would be a lack of control on the execution of elected idea and probably the same drawback as current system, where a (s)elected person does not execute the expected actions (implementing another idea than the one voters have decided).

Voter versus Citizen

During discussions on GBI, some underlined that many people today (probably the majority of voter) are and will never fill concerned by the political activity of their country. This would make the corum of 50% an idea should have to be elected difficult or impossible to reach. To cope with this, we have to distinguish citizens from voters. For a citizen to become a voter several points should be necessary:

  • Will expression to become a voter by actively regitering as voter to an adhoc state organism
  • the payment of a small fee at registration time (no more than 1/1000 of the minimum legal
    • salary, for example),

The only cause of refuse that a citizen could be opposed could be the same reasons an citizen today cannot become a voter (age, conviction for corruption,...).

Once a citizen has registered as voter, he will have the right to vote but also the duty to be an active elector. This means to perform a minimum number of actions per registration period (1 year for example) not to become forfeited of his voting right an losing the status of voter. Only voter would have the right to vote, propose idea or postulate for a role. If losing this right, a citizen could register again paying a majorated fee (twice the fee of what was payed at the previous registration, for example). The actions that could count a citizen as an active voter and thus that would avoid him to be forfeited of his voting right, could be of course the fact to vote for or against an idea, the fact to be candidate for a role of minister, the fact of being part of the ministerial cabinet, being president, having proposed an idea that have been retained, and so on. If a citizen only votes, then the minimum number of votes must be based on what a working citizen can reasonably do on his personal time, like voting for at least 100 ideas per month. Which means for example, voting for a super-idea that depends on 99 other idea or subscribing to a "tag" (see political party below) which associated organism proceeds to the tagging of at least 100 ideas per month). Of course, if a voter is unable to vote for health reasons of force majeure and can justify is situation for a give period of time, the minimum number of required votes to stay an elector will be reduced pro rata temporis.

What must contain an Idea

An idea is not just an idea, it must define means that will be provided to implement it

Objective

The objective describes the idea by itself. For example "reduce the maximum authorized speed for motorized vehicles to 110 km/h on motorways" is an objective. However this ideas implies defining penalties to driver that would be caught above the speed, provide means to indicate the maximum speed, measure the impact of the speed reduction on pollution, people health, economy and so on.

The objective of a super-idea must be coherent with its child ideas.

Risk: one person or an organism could hide some ideas in the dependency tree of ideas a super-idea can have, that implement the opposite of the super-idea itslef, leading people to be trumped. A penalty system against idea submitter would be difficult to be defined and easy to workaround by the coordination of several persons. Though the fact the idea submitter and the minister are different persons should reduce such risk of abuse. In such occurrence if the minister can demonstrate that the super-idea he has to implement lists ideas that lead to the opposite action of the super-idea, he could be granted to not implement this sub-idea. The minister will issue a idea extension (see below) to redefine the perimeter of the super idea explaining why he wants to remove certain sub-idea(s). The voters for this idea will be notified of this idea extension and will have to vote for or against this extension, as well as those that explicitly voted against the super-idea. However voter that abstained would not get the right to participate to this idea extension.

Time span

An idea must define a maximum duration of execution, it must also define the frequency of action status reports the minister would have to provide. Once the maximum duration has passed the minister ends his role whatever is the status of the idea, unless he has proposed an idea extension that has been accepted and that contains a increase of the idea duration. After that time the implementation evaluation process starts.

Budget

Each idea should provide a budget for its implementation. A second and separated budget (compensation budget) defines the amount of money allocated to the minister and his team (see Ministerial Cabinet below) for the whole time span of this idea. An third budget will also be allocated for sensors. An idea submitter cannot reduce the amount redeeming the censors below a minimum level, it can however define a higher amount if he considers that the risk is higher that selected minister would not respect the expected goal. At the opposite, the compensation budget for minister can be zero, but maybe then, no candidate will postulate for such idea as interesting as it can be for the society.

A super-idea can have his own budget, but it must also list the sum of all budgets of the child ideas recursively for eacjh voter to see the global cost of what the super-idea.

Perimeter

The idea must define a perimeter outside of which the action should not apply (in our example this might be that wind or human powered vehicles will not be concerned by this speed limitation law).

Last an Idea ID is attributed by the system once the idea has been duly registered. Such ID must be chosen for the distance between two IDs differs from at least three characters (a typo of one or two characters should not lead to a valid ID). For the same reason, only either the O letter or 0 digit, la lettre l (lowercase L) or the digit 1, and so on, should be used inside idea identifiers.

Ministerial Cabinet

A minister may appoint other persons (experts, specialists,...) in order to get help to addressing his goal. He will then share with them the compensation budget equally during their time of presence. These persons will constitute the Ministerial Cabinet. The minister has all power to modify his cabinet at any time. The budget will be shared prorata temporis of presence in the Ministerial Cabinet.

Risk: a minister could delegate his role to a so called expert that will bypass the random selection system of candidates. In the other hand a minister cannot be hampered in its action for the pretext he engages a expert that takes over his role... Puppet politician already exist today, how to reduce this risk here?

Ideas extension

It may arise that some idea get almost realized but not completely and having the same minister and ministerial cabinet to completely achieve them would make more sense that performing again the whole process of idea election. In that situation, an idea-extension can be submitted by the minister. All voter for the original idea will get notified by such idea extension, but still a majority of electors will have to vote for it for the extension to be granted. Elector that explicitly voted against such idea will also be notified, but not the other voters.

Idea extension may concern time span extension and/or budget extension and/or perimeter extension. It will have to be justified in order for the voter to understand and agree with it. The censor may amend or annotate the explanation of the minister, he may also trigger the justice if the extension differs too much from the original idea. The exposition delay is, here too, defined by the idea submitter (here the minister), to find a good balance between the remaining time to realize the idea and the necessary time to obtain the necessary vote quorum. If elected, such idea extension does not imply any candidate selection, this is the minister that will implement it.

Idea extension can also be used by the minister when there is conflict between a sub-idea and a super-idea or between ideas of a tree he has the charge to implement as described above. This may lead to the withdrawal of a sub-idea that would differ completely from the goal of the voted super-idea.

End of execution

A minister and the associated censors end their role once the maximum duration time for the idea has ended. Minister or censor may also resign in which case they will have to refund the money they received for their role, unless major force (decease, diseases duly validated,...)

the elector that have been voting for this idea or super-idea then get notified and can provide a mark on the way the idea has been executed. This notation will increase or decrease the minister selection weight which will either reduce or increase its chance to be selected again as minister if he postulates for a minister role in the future.

Tax System

Citizens

The budget sum of all ideas accepted during a given year is added to the tax amount that will be applied to citizens the year after. An idea that spans several years will have a proportional fraction of its budget allocated each year. Each idea may also define the way its budget will be allocated over time (for example 80 % the first year, and 5 % the four following years).

The way to balance this taxation between people (what we will call taxation model) based or not on their revenue, patrimony, age, volunteering in a general interest cause... is outside the scope of the GBI.

When voting for an idea, voters will see the cost they will have to pay for this idea, in regard to the existing taxation model and their position in that model. They will thus be able to have an approximation of their tax amount for the following year, which they could take into consideration to define the way they vote to come during the year.

It should be provisioned enough money to address the budget in regard to past years expenses and currently running ideas. If provision is not large enough, firstly voted ideas of the year will be active, the others will stay pending for budget acquisition or, at the will of the concerned minister, will start without fund or with privately owned funds, reimbursed later to the minister. The state has not to carry any interest load of a loan the minister could have contracted waiting for the availability of the funds to execute the idea he has the charge to execute. A pending idea is thus one that has been elected but has not yet started, its execution time is shifted up to the time its funds will be allocated.

Risk: the lack of provision may lead idea submissions to take place at a given time of the year to have more chance to get a budget sooner, leading to a load peak for voters which in return would lead the obtention of the 50% voter quorum difficult to obtain for ideas during this period and thus should counterbalance this load peak.

Companies

As companies have no right to vote for ideas, they shall not be taxed to to provide the budget of any idea. However companies do consume resources (natural and human resources) as well as public services (transportation, police, justice...) that the society provides them. They should thus be taxed regarding the use they do of natural and human resources in particular regarding the cost of resource recycling they consume for their activity and for their public services usage.

Natural Resources

For natural resources, a company will be taxed proportionally to the recycling cost of the product they build/transform. This implies that the company provides information about the transformation applied to the natural resources or already transformed resources used as base of their production, in order to estimate the cost of recycling.

Risk: rely on foreign suppliers to do the ugliest part of resource transformation, for that reason, importations should also be taxed in regard to the recycling cost of the imported products.

The state structure that has to be setup to evaluate the recycling cost of products (expert, laboratories, buildings, operating costs, controls, and so on) will be completely paid by enterprises and divided proportionnaly to the paid tax on product recycling. This way ecological enterprises will carry a smaller part of the cost than big polluters.

Following the same principle as VAT, a "RCAT" for Recycling Cost Added Tax, could address this cost, usually left to the society by bankrupted companies. By natural resource, it must also be take into account the surface used for buildings and the reference for such tax would be the destruction and redevelopment cost to get back to pre-human activity status (status the land had before the human even have used it): the cost of removing all building, concrete, roads, soil pollution, and so on.

The value of an agricultural field when no chemical product has been used (for example) during decades and soil pollution is absent can be considered equivalent (thus maximal) to pre-human activity status. Forests that have been managed for long-term during centuries can also be assimilated to this highest level of value as there is no recycling cost: just wait for the vegetation to grow in order to get back to pre-human status equivalent. This is not the case for a ruined building as one can wait for plants to grow, once trees will get their maximum height, the building and the traces of human activities will still be visible, and/or soil pollution will still have measurable consequences on flora and fauna, for example.

In consequence, a company that recycles resources and products will get compensated accordingly to the cost the civil society would have taxed a company doing the opposite action. For example a company which action is to recycle garbage will get paid according the difference of value from the garbage and the resulting recycled product. Such company will still have the cost of the transformation process but will benefit of the added value of their recycled products on the market.

Adding the recycling cost to product manufacturing should lead companies to select the best raw materials and best transformation process that ease recycling.

Human Resources

For human resource, recycling means maintaining employability of workers by mean of training, and/or role change. What should count in this evaluation is not the salary, but the difference of employability of an employee when he entered the company with its employability when he left it.

The state should compute yearly statistics for a meaningful set of role categories and publish for each role:

  • The average unemployment duration, referred as UNEMP in the following
  • The average employment duration, referred as EMP
  • The average annual salary, referred SAL in the following

We can then define the Employability Ratio (ER) for a role category by:

ER(role) = EMP / (UNEMP + EMP)

This is the average fraction of time a person having this role is employed

The difference of employability when the employee enters and leaves a company gives the variation of the fraction of time an employee will stay unemployed. The cost to the company is the Employability Tax (ET) which is defined by the product of Employability ratio variation times the average unemployment times duration times the average salary for the role employee had:

ET = [ ER(entrance role) - ER(exit role) ] * UNEMP(exit role) * SAL(exit role)

This can mean the company will receive money for the ET if the employee has increased its employability on the market:

But of course this also means that if a company does not take care of maintaining employability of its employees, it will have to pay a tax according to the decrease of ER:

It means a company does not carry the load of unemployment for a given employee that leaves the company, but only the degradation of value an employee had on the market by staying in the company. And if the ER value increased during the time the employee stood in the company, this one would be reward for that.

But, to avoid a company to artificially change the role of employees before firing them and this way avoid paying for the ET, during the average duration time of the new role a penalty ER is introduced which lead the ET to be the following:

  • First the ET reward expressed above should be prorata temporis of the employee time in this new role compared to the average employee duration for that role.
  • Second we introduce the notion of Penalty ER, which corresponds to the ER the employee had N years before his role change, where N is the duration the employee has spent in its new role (see diagram below). The difference of ER compared to the entrance ER times gives a second ET that is added to the first ET prorata temporis to the complement of the first ET time ratio:

That way just after the change of role the benefit of the role change is little because the time spent in this new role is little (prorata temporis). At the same time the Penalty ER is the one the employee has in his former role just before changing of role, thus the penalty ET is maximum because the time ratio for penalty is also maximum. This is as if the employee had left without having changed of role. As time passes and as the employee has more experience in this new role, the benefit ER increases and the penalty ER decreases both because the Penalty ER decreases in value but also because the time ratio for it is reduced as time passes. After EMP time in the new role, the time ratio for the penalty ET is null and the ET is equal to the ET calculated previously only considering the entrance ER and the exit ER (see examples below)

It is assumed the indemnity provided to unemployed people is proportional to the average salary for the role the employee had in his previous professional experience (and not linked to its real own salary). The cost of unemployment is charged on all salaries of active persons, sort of mandatory insurance for unemployment. As a consequence the unemployment cost is paid by the citizen only, the company pays only the loss of value of their employee when they leave, this should lead company to provide training to their people that have real value on the market for their employee and also promote them when a given job is decreasing.

Why not making difference when an employee resign and when he is fired? Because it may leave due to pressure from the employer.

Why not making difference when an employee resigns for a new job? Because it does not change the need for a company to maintaining employability. This does not forbid company to contract conditions (if conform to law) for the employee to engage for a given period of time in the company for the new role.

Last the ET should be calculated each year and redeemed or charged to companies each year, as if all their employee were leaving at the end of year and were arrived at the end of the previous year. This way it takes care of company bankruptcy.

The running cost of such institution that evaluate UNEMP, EMP, SAL for all professional categories the buildings, the people that work there, ... are carried by the enterprises under the form of an added tax proportionnel to the number of employee the each company had during the year (partial time employee count prorata temporis compared to a full time employment)

Example 1:

Mr Doe has been employed for 10 years in company A always in the same role category, which at the time he entered the company had the following indicators: UNEMP = 1 year, EMP = 5 years, SAL = 60 k€. After these 10 years, Mr Doe leaves or is fired by company A, but the role indicators are now: UEMP = 2 years, EMP = 5 years, SAL 50 k€
The ER at entrance was: 5 / (5 + 1) = 0.83
The ER at exit is: 5 / (5 + 2) = 0.71
The ET for enterprise A is: (0.83 - 0.71) * 2 * 50 k€ = +12 k€

But if the indicator for this role had been better (unemployment duration reduced for example) company A would have received money accordingly, without having done anything!

We see that the unemployment indemnity for the former employee will be 100 k€ in average (for two years), this is not the company that pays this amount, company only pays the variation of cost the unemployment for this role has between the time the employee has been engaged and the time the employee leaves the company.

Example 2:

Mr Doe has started at company B as role 1 (UEMP = 1, EMP = 3, SAL = 50 k€) after 5 years, role 1 indicators are (UEMP = 2, EMP = 3, SAL = 45 k€) at that time company B to avoid the corresponding ET cost decides to promote Mr Doe to role 2 with a salary of 50 k€ (no salary change) eventually providing him an adapted training for this new role . After 6 years in this role 2, which we assume has not changed significantly over years (UEMP = 1, EMP = 6, SAL = 70 k€) Mr Doe resigned for another company.
The ER at entrance was: 3 / (3 + 1) = 0.75
The ER at exit is: 6 / (6 + 1) = 0.85

As Mr. Doe has had an experience duration greater or equal the EMP in his last role (6 years), the penalty ER is ignored and the company is rewarded the following ET:
ET = (0.75 - 0.85) * 1 * 70 k€ = -7 k€

But if Mr Doe had left or was fired after one year instead of 6, the penalty ER would have played:
Penalty ER = 3/(3 + 2) = 0.6
The ET would have been:
ET = ET1 + ET2
ET1 = (0.75 - 0.85) * 1 * 70 k€ * 1/6 = -1,16 k€
ET2 = (0.75 - 0.6) * 2 * 45 k€ * 5/6 = +11,25 k€

Where from the total ET = +10 k€

Company B would have to pay 10 k€ for the Employability Tax. This mechanism avoid the company to try reducing the ET by changing the role category of employee just before firing them. The fact that Mr Doe only stood one year while the average employment duration is 6 years also means this experience at role 2 is too short to have a complete value and Mr. Doe may experiment difficulties being engaged under this role in the future.

Costs of Risks

If an employee dies due to risk inherent to the role he has or due to lack of security addressed by the company, this one will have to redeem the heirs to the last salary the employee had for the time remaining to his retirement. Any membership fee for retirement shall be performed as if the person was still alive and would provide a retired amount up to the average life expectancy for the role the employee had. Proving the origin of the injuries would be a decision done by the Justice (nothing automatic here are their are ways to abuse the system by employee as well as employers)

For that Reason Company should have insurance for any risk they can bring to the resources, whether human or natural. In particular an Industrial company that manipulate dangerous chemical substance that could explode or pollute the grounds should contract an insurance in regard to this risks and this way be able to fix and compensate any catastrophe which could be generated by its activity. This system should also limit the occurrence of company filing for bankruptcy after a such catastrophe due to the colossal charges it would cause.

Use of public Services

Companies should also be taxed in regard to their use of transportation infrastructure, common and public services, as well as for regalian services. If for the first ones the tax can be proportional to the use (transportation, postal services when publicly owned...) care should be taken for the access to Justice to be possible even for little or emerging companies. The tax could be for example indexed on the turnover that each company had the previous year (no tax for the first year of exercise of a company). Note that losing a pursuit does not mean having no cost for such small companies. In other words, each company will have to pay a tax for justice which amount will depend on their turnover whatever they are suing or not.

Controlling system

The cost of controlling (real use of natural and human resource, adequate risks insurance subscribed and proper declaration of public services usage) must be carried by the companies' activities. This cost can be balanced between companies taking into account each company's turnover, here again. The fine amount in case of infraction should be chose high enough to compensate the lack of risk to be caught by the controlling system. Less tax implies less means for the controlling system resources, thus the fine amount in case of fraud must be increased in consequence for dissuasion: in other words, the product of the fine amount times the frequency of controls or difficulty to be caught, must be constant. Note that the amount of fines is not used to carry the functionning of this controlling system, but to cover the cost that would have been left to the civil society if such controlling system was not present. The functionning cost of the controlling system is independent from the fraud and is funded by enterprises.

Constitution

Once an idea has been retained but before a minister get selected, it must be validated for conformance with the constitution. A constitutional council is needed for that and can be solicited by the president of the executive power, president of the justice power and by some associations recognized of social interest (like association acting against corruption, for human rights...).

The constitutional council can also give a status without external solicitation. In case of rejection of an idea, the constitutional council must justify its decision based on the constitution. If an idea is rejected by this council this will lead it to the same fate as if it was not elected.

The definition of a constitution is outside the scope of GBI, though one could allow the GBI system to add, modify or remove rules of the constitution, it should be done with some additional validation process outside the perimeter of this proposal (higher ratio of voter for this constitutional idea...)

Executive Power - President Role and interaction

As stated in introduction, the GBI in addition to be a complete legislative power (unicameral) also partial covers the executive power that needs to be completed by a presidential role as of today in many democracies (directly or indirectly elected by voters). The frontier between actions that the president can take and those that he cannot take alone should be defined precisely by the constitution and is out of the scope of this proposal. However, some decision the president has the power to take alone could still have the form of a presidential idea with maybe smaller quorum of elector necessary for its adoption (relative majority of voter with 33 % of favorable voters for example). Also a dedicated budget could be defined in advanced for such situation and needed actions. Such idea will be implemented the same as other ideas: by candidate selection not by the president or by its cabinet.

Last, some actions and responsibilities should stay under the exclusive responsibility of the president and not be managed nor validated through GBI (nuclear power, weapon use, war, etc.).

Referendum

Referendum are just a particular case of submitting a idea to the GBI system. Electors as well as the executive power can propose idea. But only presidential ideas can lead to a mandatory voting from all electors.

Execution Conflicts

It may arise that two elected ideas enter in conflict. For example, a first elected idea would tends to increase security of car drivers in case of accident by introducing a geolocation automated devices in vehicles that would detect the occurrence of an accident and trigger emergency alert, while another elected idea (possibly already completed) would for example control the personal information collected from people including their geolocation all along the time.

For that reason a set of prime ministers has to be selected (randomly from a list of candidates and for a fixed duration) to arbitrate between ideas in conflict and define their respective perimeter of application.

Instead of one single person, having an odd number of prime ministers randomly selected in a list of candidates (same criteria as for minister to postulate to this role) sould reduce the risk of arbitrary decision.

The prime ministers action should only take place when a minister claims a conflict against another idea. They should modify each idea perimeter in order for the conflict to disappear. Only 3 prime minister will be randomly selected to arbitrate a conflict (more could harm the effectiveness of the decision). But a broader set of prime-minister can be selected in regard to the expected load of arbitration they will have to address.

During prime-minister resolution conflict, concerned ideas are suspended, this means their time span is extended, but their budget stays the same.

Risk: There is the risk a party provides under his master super-idea a set of useless ideas that could be used to trigger a conflict with other ideas that they are against. This could undermine idea with little compensation budget due to the fact the minister would not be compensated during the conflict resolution.

Random selection

Candidacy conditions

As described above, minister, censors and prime-ministers have to be randomly chosen from a list of candidates. We also saw that postulating to any of these roles implies to respect some conditions:

  • Only voters can postulate to any of these roles
  • One cannot postulate to any of these roles, if it has ever been condemned for corruption, no prescription.
  • one cannot postulate to the role of censor if it has been condemned three times for censorship abuse.
  • one cannot postulate to the role of prime-minister if it has been condamned three times for censorship abuse.

For all roles, we could also add some conditions, like to not be in prison, and so on.

Weighted candidacy

Now we should also valuable successful role execution, by mean of weights given to each candidate. Each voter having postulated at least once for a role would receive a weight which value would be 1 initially. This weight would increase for successes and decrease for failures of executions. The random selection of candidates takes care or relative weight of all candidates in order to provide proportionnal chances to be selected according to the weight of each candidate.

For example, to select between 3 candidates or respective weights 1, 3 and 6, the random selection will randomly choose a number from 1 to 10. if the value 1 is drawn, candidate 1 is selected. If one of the value 2, 3 or 4 is drawn, candidate 2 is selected. If one of the values 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 or 10 is drawn, candidate 3 is selected for the role.

Weight Evolution for a Minister

As briefly introduced above, the initial weight for a candidate is 1. At the end of idea implementation, voters for this idea are asked to give a rating about their satisfaction about this implemented (voters than voted against explictly or implicitly are excluded from this step). This rating can be the usual 5 values one (1 - totally unsatisfied to 5 - totally satisfied). From the average rating M (which is a floating point number between 1 and 5) a weight multiplicator V is derived from the formula V = 2^(M-3). For example:

  • V is 1/4 if the average rating is 1 (lowest) the candidate weight will be divided by 4
  • V is 1/2 if the average rating is 2: the candidate weight will be divided by 2
  • V is 1 if the average rating is 3: the candidate weight is unchanged
  • V is 2 if the average rating is 4: the candidate weight is doubled
  • V is 4 if the average rating is 5: the candidate weight is quadrupled

Of course all intermediate values of M will give intermediate values for V according the the formula mentionned previously.

This way, minister that could show good skills in execution are more likely to be selected for a next idea they would postulate for. While getting a poor rating would not eliminate the former minister but let newcomer having more place to "take their chance" for a future selection.

Weighting Censors

Another weight than the one associated to the minister role, as seen above can be used to keep trace of the censors actions. For example, a censor that could demonstrate the corruption or result falsification of a minister and lead him condemned could have would have his censor weight multiplied by 10.

But a censor that would have be condemned for censorship abuse would have its weight divided by 5 for each such sentence received (up to 3 in his life).

Weighting Prime Ministers

The role of prime minister is difficult to evalute for several reasons:

  • a prime minister never acts alone but in a cohort of 3 prime ministers
  • The decision they take is always partial, and may never satisfy perfectly all ministers concerned by a conflict (Else, there would not be a conflit to solve)

However, it could be possible to let the minister that have been in conflict for their idea to give a rating to the group of three ministers they have been arbitrated by. We can use the same mecanism to translate from rating to weight multiplicator as for ministers evaluations by electors. if a conflit between ideas is has been arbitrated in a completely unfair manner, the resulting rating will most probably be 3 (average of 1 and 5), thus the weight multiplicator of 1 would not change the prime minister rating.

Use a different system that would reduce the weight of prime ministers in such situation would expose them to blackmail for rating from ministers. At the opposite, prime ministers that would have satisfied positively both parties in conflit even partially and thus would have found a good compromise would get better rating than for the unfaire arbitration we just saw and would be favored compared to this first case. For example, a conflit for which leads to a rating of 3 and 4 will give an average of 3.4 which translates into a weigh factor of 1.4 approximatively.

Doing that way, leverages candidates with good skills for a given role. Professional politician could well emerge, but should be sanctioned the same as newcomers and be deprived from their right to postulate if they get condemned for corruption, abuse... even if their weight had increased a lot over time.

This different from today's professional politicians is that they are elected based on their ability to let people believe in what they promise today and their ability to have people forget what they promised yesteday. The proposed system here would for sure keep professional politician for their ability to satisfy voters and thus respect what they voted for. Would that make worse professional politicians as of today?

Secrecy and control of the random selection process

The random seletion process briefly introduced above brings challenge to address:

  • guarantee fair selection and the freedom from fraud by fraud
  • address scalability that will be need as it may be expected a lot of ideas, ministers, censors and prime minister to be selected over time

A first approach could be to have loto-like election, this would allow any candidate for an election as well as any elector to be able to assist to the selection process. It could even be broadcasted to public media. Each selection must be performed under the control of a bailiff and would use a mechanical machine (not computer science based here, as there is no way to control from the outside the fairness of the random selection from the outside). A statistical control can however be made about the randomness of the generated serie of number such mecanism generates, but this dos not guarantee that the system has not been tampered and that from time to time it generates a number that is the one asked by an external source of information. The problem here is also cost and scalability of such system.

To address these points, we could rather rely on a two levels selection process and use computer science to provide seamless automated election without compromising the randomness of the selection process by doing the following way:

  • A first organism, the submitter, assigns candidates to one or several numbers in an range rearding their respective weight. The assignation is not important here, it may be the order the candidates have registered for example.
  • Each candidate receives from the submitter organism a ciphered message containing the number(s) he has been assigned to, but without the key to uncipher it. This key will be sent by the submitter to a second organism the generator, which does not know the numbers assigned to the candidates. The generator will also be provide the reference for this selection (idea ID, etc.) with the information of range in which to randomly draw a number.
  • Once the generator has randomly selected the number in the requested range, it publishes the selected number beside the selection reference and the secret key it received from the submitter for that selection and which permits to uncipher all messages sent by the submitter to the candidates.
  • the submitter organism reading the result designates the selected candidate and all candidates with the knownledge of the secret key now published by the generator, can be sure the submitter has not reassigned numbers knowing the result from the generator organism.
  • The submitter organism and any citizen including the candidates will also be able to control the randomness of the number issued by the generator by computing statistics of all selections performed over time, this to avoid the submitter, having detected some inconsistency in the randomness, to assign some candidates to some numbers that could be more likely to be drawn.

For the voting system example provided in annex, the EIB could be the submitter organism and the VA could be the generator organism. The inverse could also be possible.

Return to civil life

Ministers

Care must be taken for ministers once they have completed their idea to be reinserted into civil life. For that reason if they can provide evidence they are actively seeking for a new job a former minister will receive a composensation up to twice the minimum legal salary and up to they time they find a new job but not for a longer time than the time they spent as minister. The amount they will receive per month ranges from the minimum to twice the minum legal salary depending on the rating they received at the end of their role as minister. If a minister resigned, he will not get any compensation.

Risk: a balance has to be found between the level of mean provided to get back to civil life and the risk to have people postulating to such role just for the money they will have during the role and the compensation they will have after to carry their return to civil life. Care must also be taken for the global cost of this feature as there may be a lot of minister in a given period of time...

Other members of the ministerial cabinet are not concerned by such compensation as they were not chosen randomly, but for their skills. As such they did not had to abandon their work and can add this experience as expert or specialist for the ministerial cabinet on their resume. This professional experience is the continuity of their day to day work and if they were successful they would have more chance than before to find a job, including a new role of expert in another ministerial cabinet.

Censors

Censors will receive the lowest legal salary at most for the duration of their activity as censor during the unemployment and if they could justify of an active search for a new job. The Censor does not take a lot of risk for his role he can also just do nothing it will be difficult to control its work, so it is difficult allocate more without a way to evaluate the work performed by censors.

Prime Ministers

At the end of their mandate prime minister will follow the same process as the minister, and obtain a variable reserve of fund to cover their period of unemployment following their role as prime minister up to a duration equal to the time spent as prime minister. The rating used will be the average of all rating received during their period of activity in this role. As before they will have to justify to be actively seeking for a job.

Political Party

By political party we mean here any group of persons sharing a common view and/or ideology, this may well be also a philosophical group, a lobby, a syndicate for examples. . Such entity can register a "tag" to the GBI system which they will be allowed to add to any existing idea, whatever is their author. Tags can be either positively or negatively applied to an idea, meaning either that the corresponding political party approves or rejects such idea. Of course such political party would tag positively the ideas they submit, but by mean of super-idea or more simple by mean of tag, a political party can approve the ideas they are not the author of but with which they agree. This avoid idea duplication and thus leads more chance for a same idea to be visibile by different types of electors and thus to be elected.

An idea can thus receive several tags from several political parties. This gives the choice to voters to define filters to list ideas in phase with their own criteria. We can imagin rich logical expression like: list ideas for which tag A or B is present and positive and tag C is absent or not negative and if the voter has no time and trusts the political parties these tags come from, vote for or against such ideas without even understanding them.

The tagging of an idea requires authentication to avoid tag usurpation.

The advantage for the electors is to not be oblided to be either in the box of a given party of in the box of another party, but to pick ideas from almost everywhere in regard to their own convictions. However political parties have a plac and can propose a set of coherent ideas in one or severl domains.

We could also imagine a system where elector can subscribe to a given set of tags (using filter based on rich logical expression as seen above) define a maximum budget and have the system voting for the ideas matching such filter(s) while its cost is below an elector defined amount and while the global elector defined budget has not been exhausted. Selected ideas by the filter that would be out of budget would be available in the tool for more attentive consideration in order for the elector to takes its own decision.

Risk: This mechanism may lead some elector to completely delegate their voice and not becoming concerned by political activity nor in the future of the country anymore. However they will vote for idea, thus in a way the would participate in the decision process of their country.

Idea submitter consideration

It could be added as an option that idea submitter get rewarded when an idea they submitted has been elected. The reward could be financial and visible as such in the total cost of the idea. This amount could even be defined by the idea submitter inside a limit range fraction of the total budget of the idea. At least the cost of the idea submission should be return to the submitter if this idea has been elected.

If this is an amendment of an idea that has been retained this reward will go for 2/3 to the original idea author and 1/3 to the author of the elected amendment, unless this one has decided a more advantageous dstribution to the author of the idea in his amendment.

Conclusion

The objective of such political system defining a new legislative power and partially redefining the executive power is to involve people closer to the decision process and letting them keep a eye on the way it is executed. There is many options (becoming or not a voter, relying on automatic tag voting) that can let people just live they political life as of today, but at least for more concerned citizen, there is room to propose, room to choose, room to control and room to act without make a full time job of politics. While traditional political party would still have their place in that system.

Annexes

Voting System Example

GBI implies frequent election for many submitted ideas. Relying on new technology is necessary to reduce cost for the society and time for the citizen. However care must be taken to provide vote privacy and control. The following is a proposal of system that is in fact independent on the GBI but is provided just to illustrate the feasibility of the frequent voting requirements of GBI.

For that purpose two independent organism can be defined:

  • Elector Identity Bureau - EIB, which registers electors and authenticate them
  • Virtual Assembly - VA which contains exposed and adopted ideas, search engine and voting system with filtering feature to help voters.

EIB

This bureau can be a centralized or distributed organism, each city for example registering its citizen based on official passport or ID card and a centralized legal register of person banned from the GBI system for corruption or other justified reasons. Its first role is to attribute an Elector ID to each electors. An Elector ID a public number with integrated checksum (to detect typo error). An Elector is also given a secret pin/password/passphrase, which the elector is the only one to know (like PIN for sim cards, credit cards, and the like). EIB may renew ID from time to time and will communicate the new ID by postal letter as of today or ciphered emails if the elector has registered a GPG key and email to the EIB portal.

EIB provides an SSL portal to the electors on Internet that has several purposes:

  • Register a GPG public key and email (after having authenticated with the Elector ID)
  • Hide the IP address of the elector terminal to the VA, and more generaly anonymize the exchanges between the elector and the VA.
  • Provide a single sign on for the VA
  • Place to report fraud about Elector ID usage

The EIB is thus the only organism having the correspondence between an Elector ID at a given time and the elector person it has been assigned to at that time. In particular the VA does only know the Elector ID and has no information about the Elector itself. At the opposite we will see that EIB has no information about the exposed or voted ideas.

A terminal used for voting by an elector (laptop, smartphone, tablet...), when connecting to the VA, has to pass through the EIB. EIB acts then as web proxy. The VA portal must not be accessible directly from Internet for interacting with electors (idea reading, filtering, voting) but a page of information should be telling how to configure the Elector terminal to vote in total privacy. VA access is ciphered by SSL, thus EIB has no way to know about the votes and actions done by the elector when the communication is established from the terminal to VA through the EIB Proxy. Initially and for a limited time, the Elector first authenticate to the EIB proxy which generates a token the terminal will use during the session with VA. The VA will receive this token and interrogate the EIB on its validity and Elector ID correspondence) and can then load the elector context (configured filters, voted ideas, and so on) and then present it to the elector through the ciphered established connection.

When the user will perform a vote on the VA, the VA will provide the token and Elector ID to the EIB, which will validate the authenticity of the Elector to the VA and the fact the token has not expired. The EIB can thus count the number of vote a given elector has performed, and control the active status of the elector. The fact the session passes through the EIB proxy hides from the VA the original IP address and enforce anonymization of Electors to the VA. In particular if the terminal is not properly configured to allow this level of anonymization to the VA, the EIB during the authentication phase must refuse the connection to the VA and provide in return to the elector a clear message about the failure and the mean to fix it.

VA

The Virtual Assembly is the place where is gathered and registered the ideas. This registration and payment is done without interacting with the EIB, submitter must be publicly identified and be registered. This is the VA idea submitter portal

The VA is also the place where --- through the EIB proxy and with EIB authentication --- votes take place. Once authenticated to the VA voter portal each elector has a view of exposed ideas including the ones he has already voted for (or explicitly against), He can also see the ideas that are currently executed (having information about minister and ministerial cabinet, censors and budget used, the action status report provided by the minister, and so on).

The VA voter portal offer the possibility to forget the ideas he has voted for or against in the past, The voter can reactivate it afterward but won‚Äôt see votes done before that time. Doing so avoids the voter to rate the minister once the idea has been completed as the voter is no more associated to this idea. It is not notified about idea extensions for past ideas and so on.

Elector should have the ability to search ideas by keywords, by end of exposing date (date at which nobody can vote for it and its vote count is evaluated), by authors, by tags and combination of those filters. The possibility to browse ideas up and down in a tree of super-idea and sub-ideas should also be available (when a super-idea contains sub-ideas the Elector rejected, he may want to vote manually for some other sub-idea except that one).

Of course if an Elector has voted against an idea, some color code could show that status for such ideas but also for the super ideas that due to that vote cannot be voted for. Same thing when voting for a super-idea all sub-idea should have color code telling the Elector has voted for implicitly. View should also allow an Elector to see not yet voted ideas only, voted for ideas or voted against ideas.

A customizable color could also be associated to filter the user would configure to focus on some type of ideas.

Once an idea has been voted for, some statistics about the number of votes are sent back to the EIB for control:

When a voter votes for an idea, EIB get solicited for Elector authentication and can count the number of votes a given Elector has done but EIB cannot know for which idea the elector has voted.

EIB can thus during a given period of time compare the total number of votes summing the number of votes per electors with the total number of votes received from VA statistics by summing the amount of voter for each idea that has been voted for during that time. These two sums should be equal in absence of fraud.

Securing Elector Terminals

The terminal or user device has to be secured. Choice must be left to the elector to define the security of his terminal by itself or to rely on vendor terminal certified as secure by the EIB or even provided by EIB. EIB should also provide good practice for a secure Election process, including social engineering menace and all that goes with (phising, etc.)

Risk: the risk is that EIB by mean of such terminal provided by itself get information of the votes done by the elector and even modifies votes done without the elector noticing.

Structural Costs

The cost of such legislative system (idea election) and executive system (minister, censors and prime-minister) is contained in the budget of the elected idea. This is thus the electors that define their level of tax and accordingly their level of services. Majority being the decision maker, should be reduce the case where a small subset of the population (a lobby or a company) passes laws that only benefit to this minority.

Remains for the state some structural costs to take into account:

  • All regalian services, even if companies contribute to the service they use (Justice in particular) based on their turnover (for example), citizens have to contribute to these services under the form of global tax that the chosen taxation will load balance between citizens. Remains to define the ratio between the global private sector contribution and the global citizens contribution
  • Constitutional council members and means, as of today
  • Prime ministers, which are not compensated from an elected idea as they have to arbitrate perimeters and priorities between them, no representation role, less budget need but more people to compensate.
  • The voting and idea submission system (and its controlling entity) EIB and VA in our example.
  • The candidate selection system and its controlling entity, which were designated by the submitter and the generator entities in this proposal. Those roles could be taken by the EIB and VA to reduce the administrative cost of theses entities.
  • A super censorship that may be elected by the people to control the censors not to have collusion with the minister they have to control
